# Kabir-sales-analysis
Sales Report Summary
Key findings

Top performer: Tola is the most consistent and highest-volume seller.

Overall trend: Total sales are declining and warrant a product-level review.

Team performance: All sales reps contribute meaningfully; expanding the team could raise total revenue if supported by process and product fixes.

Analysis
Concentration risk: Heavy reliance on Tola creates vulnerability if performance dips.

Decline drivers (likely): product-market fit issues, pricing, inventory or fulfillment problems, weaker marketing, or seasonal effects.

Hiring impact: Adding reps can increase capacity, but without addressing product and process issues, new hires may amplify inefficiencies and raise costs.

Recommendations
Immediate (0–30 days)

Run a product review to identify declining SKUs, margin changes, and customer feedback.

Segment sales decline by product, region, and channel to pinpoint causes.

Protect revenue: replicate Tola’s best practices (scripts, accounts, territories) across the team.

Short term (30–60 days)

Fix quick wins from the product review (pricing, promotions, stockouts).

Implement targeted coaching and share Tola’s playbook; set short-term KPIs.

Pilot hiring 1–2 reps on a contract basis tied to clear ramp metrics.
